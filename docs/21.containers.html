Containers represent a major evolutionary step in the history of system virtualization. --- name: conclusions class: center, middle # A Brief History of Virtualization --- ## Bare Metal Machines In old times, a server was a physical device affectionately called a "box" or a "machine". These boxes, like your personal computers, typically had one set of hardware, an operating system, and some applications installed on it. [!A bare metal machine](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Finsurancehub.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2016%2F09%2Flaptop-fire-insurancehub.jpg&f=1&nofb=1) --- ## Blade Servers Computer hardware, loaded with the operating system and applications, was eventually fit onto a single hardware card. Many such cards are slotted into a single "box", reducing space and sharing a single power supply for multiple machines. [!A blade server](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.rackmountmart.com%2Fprodspics%2Frm8003-01.jpg&f=1&nofb=1) --- ## Virtual Machines With increases in computing power, an entire machine - hardware and software included - could be virtualized, allowing one physical machine to run many simulated virtual machines. Virtual machines have protections against one consuming the resources of another. --- ## Containers Containers are a refinement of the concept of a virtual machine.
